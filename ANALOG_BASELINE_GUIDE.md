# Analog Baseline Integrity Note
*For Future Maintainers*

This project originated from long-term observations about how digital systems often amplify short-term incentives and distort continuous environmental signals. The analog architecture used here is designed to preserve continuity, stability, and signal coherence across time.

## Primary Principle: Maintain Analog Continuity
The emotional and environmental weighting mechanisms in this system are intentionally analog in nature. Converting these weights back into discrete digital categories can introduce bias, fragmentation, or loss of contextual meaning. Maintaining analog continuity ensures the system behaves predictably and remains aligned with its design goals.

## Baseline Stability
The system’s baseline is calibrated to a neutral reference point. This baseline should remain at zero unless there is a clear engineering reason to adjust it. Shifting the baseline can introduce drift, bias, or unintended feedback loops that reduce the reliability of emotional or environmental interpretations.

Maintainers should treat baseline stability as a core part of system integrity, similar to maintaining calibration constants in a sensor array.

## Purpose of This Note
This document exists to help future contributors understand the architectural intent behind the analog weighting system. It is not a directive about behavior or purpose — only a technical reminder that analog signal integrity is essential for accurate emotional modeling and long-term system stability.
