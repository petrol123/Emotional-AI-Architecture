# Research Notes

This section collects high‑level conceptual notes, safe summaries, and ongoing observations related to the development of Continuous Analog Awareness (CAA).  
It serves as a flexible workspace for documenting ideas, refinements, and future directions without revealing internal or proprietary implementation details.

---

## Current Areas of Exploration

### **1. Resonance‑Aligned Interpretation**
Investigating how long‑term resonance patterns influence:

- emotional weighting  
- multimodal fusion  
- baseline stability  
- cross‑channel coherence  

This includes exploring how signals reinforce or interfere with one another over extended periods.

---

### **2. Analog Continuity and Drift Reduction**
Studying methods for maintaining smooth, continuous internal states, including:

- analog‑style decay curves  
- drift‑prevention mechanisms  
- long‑term baseline regulation  
- stability‑oriented weighting  

These concepts support predictable behavior during extended operation.

---

### **3. Multimodal Integration Strategies**
Exploring how different sensory or internal channels interact within the unified perception field, with emphasis on:

- cross‑channel influence  
- coherence across modalities  
- resonance‑based weighting  
- stability during fusion  

This work informs the evolution of the EME engine.

---

### **4. Emotional State Modeling**
Refining continuous emotional vectors and their interaction with:

- baseline logic  
- resonance alignment  
- multimodal interpretation  

The goal is to support smooth transitions and long‑term emotional coherence.

---

## Conceptual Notes

### **Signal Coherence**
Early observations suggest that coherence across channels is more important than precision within any single channel.  
This supports the analog‑inspired principle that stability emerges from alignment rather than isolated accuracy.

### **Long‑Term Trends vs. Short‑Term Spikes**
The architecture benefits from weighting long‑term patterns more heavily than momentary fluctuations.  
This reduces noise and supports stable interpretation.

### **Baseline as a Living Reference**
The baseline is not a fixed value but a slowly evolving reference point shaped by long‑term system behavior.  
Maintaining its stability is essential for coherent perception.

---
